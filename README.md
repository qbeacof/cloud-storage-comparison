## **Why People Start Searching for a TeraBox Alternative**

TeraBox's pitch is simple and genuinely tempting: 1TB of free cloud storage, no credit card required. For a casual user who just wants to dump a few photos, that's hard to beat on paper. The friction shows up the moment you push the service past light use.

**The real-world pain points, based on user reports across Reddit, YouTube reviews, and forums:**

- **Aggressive download throttling.** Free users frequently report speeds dropping to 1–2 Mbps, with 400 HTTP errors and connection drops at peak times. Files that should take minutes take hours.
- **Single-file download limits.** Free accounts can only download one file at a time, and large files often require jumping through hoops (third-party downloaders, browser extensions) just to pull them at a usable speed.
- **Ad-heavy free tier.** The mobile app experience is punctuated by full-screen ads, and the web interface pushes upgrade prompts at every step.
- **File size caps on free uploads.** Free users hit a 4GB per-file ceiling, which rules out most HD movies and large archives.
- **Privacy and data-residency questions.** TeraBox is operated by FlexBerry, and there's ongoing community discussion about where data is stored and how it's handled. For users storing personal media, that's a real consideration.
- **Region inconsistency.** Performance varies by geography, and some users report access issues depending on location.

The takeaway: 1TB free is real, but "free" comes packaged with enough friction that serious users start hunting for alternatives within weeks.
